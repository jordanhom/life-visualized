Why do some years show 53 weeks?

A year has 365 days (52 weeks + 1 day) or 366 in a leap year (52 weeks + 2 days). That extra day or two makes things interesting!

  • Calendar View: Uses the official ISO 8601 week numbering standard (weeks start Monday). Due to how weeks are numbered around New Year's, some calendar years officially contain 53 weeks.
  • Age View: Shows all the standard Monday-Sunday weeks you actually live through during that year of your age (from birthday to birthday). Because a year isn't exactly 52 weeks long and your birthday falls on a different weekday each year, the period usually overlaps with 53 different standard weeks.
